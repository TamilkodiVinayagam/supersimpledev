Healthy Rasam

Rasam is a traditional South Indian soup or thin stew that's known for its tangy and spicy flavors. It's typically enjoyed as a side dish with rice in South Indian cuisine. Rasam is often served as part of a larger meal and is especially popular during festivals and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • tamarind juice
  • tomatoes
  • black pepper
  • cumin
  • mustard seeds
  • mustard seeds
  • curry leaves

The preparation of rasam involves a few basic steps:

  1. Tamarind pulp or tamarind concentrate is mixed with water to create a tangy tamarind base for the rasam.
  2. Chopped tomatoes are added to the tamarind water and boiled until they break down and blend into the liquid.
  3. A mixture of spices including black pepper, cumin, and sometimes red chili is ground to a paste. This spice blend adds heat and flavor to the rasam.
  4. Mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and sometimes fenugreek seeds are tempered in hot oil or ghee. Curry leaves and dried red chilies are often added to the tempering for extra flavor.
  5. The spice paste and tempering are added to the tamarind-tomato mixture. The rasam is then simmered, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  6. Chopped fresh coriander leaves are added at the end to provide a burst of freshness.
